## Honest framing (the through-line for the companion)

A RAID log is a **consolidation convention, not a standard**. There is **no documented inventor and no
governing body**: PRINCE2 7th edition does not name it, PMBOK does not define it, and even the acronym is
genuinely contested - the **A** is Assumptions or Actions, the **D** is Dependencies or Decisions, and the
UK's Association for Project Management and the Institute of Risk Management use different expansions **in
their own materials**. This bundle teaches the RAID log that survives its two defining failures: the
**acronym ambiguity** (the fix is "pick one expansion, write it down, stop relitigating it") and
**"documentation theater"** (set up at kickoff, abandoned by week three; the fix is a real review cadence and
one named owner per item).

**Sharpest teaching points:**
1. The four quadrants are **genuinely different kinds of thing** with different fields and cadences: a
   **Risk** might happen; an **Assumption** is believed true without proof; an **Issue** has already
   happened; a **Dependency** is a structural relationship. They do not share a schema.
2. The unifying insight is **quadrant migration**: an assumption that fails becomes an issue; a dependency
   that slips becomes a risk, then an issue; a risk that materializes becomes an issue. The log's value is
   seeing these hand-offs in one place.
3. The RAID log is the **weekly working consolidation**; the `risk-register` is the **deepened R** for
   governance. The RAID's R can point to the register rather than duplicate it.
4. An **assumption is a latent risk in disguise** - the "impact if false" and "validation date" fields exist
   to catch its decay before it becomes an issue.
5. An **issue has no probability** (it has happened); it carries a raised-date for aging instead of a trigger.
6. A **dependency has a direction** (inbound / outbound) and a party; that is what drives its escalation path.
7. One **named owner per item**, never a team; the PM owns the log, the team populates it.

**The honest-retrieval landmine:** do not assert a single canonical RAID expansion or a founding source.
There is none. State the ambiguity and the "pick one" discipline.

## Claims flagged contested or time-bound

- **No canonical RAID expansion, no founding source** [1][3][4][9][16]. The A is Assumptions or Actions; the
  D is Dependencies or Decisions; RAAIDD keeps more. The IRM [1] and APM [4] use different expansions in
  their own materials. The companion states the ambiguity and the "pick one, write it down" discipline [9].
- **PRINCE2 7th does not name the RAID log** [2]; it is a practitioner overlay, not a standards-mandated
  artifact. Stated as such.
- **"A=Assumptions/D=Dependencies" vs "A=Actions/D=Decisions"** [3][9][16] is a real, unresolved practitioner
  split, with a functional argument on each side (passive-monitoring items vs actively-managed items [3]).
- **Should agile teams keep a RAID log?** [20][23][24]. The anti-pattern camp [20] calls it a PMI/PMO
  blame instrument; the mainstream [11][24] treats it as accountability infrastructure. Presented as a live
  debate, mainstream leaning.
- **Should Assumptions and Dependencies be their own quadrants or folded into Risks?** [3] (fold) vs [19]
  (keep). Presented both ways.
- **Severity vs priority for issues** [14] (distinct) vs many templates (merged). Noted as a choice.
- **One combined table vs four separate tabs** [17] (combined) vs [7][16] (tabs). Context-dependent; stated.
- **The "78% of UK government projects" origin statistic is unverified** and is NOT used anywhere in this
  bundle; the source page cited an APM 2023 document that could not be located.
